Volapük Fasilik in 10 simple rules:


1 - Phonetics

There are 8 vowels in Volapük. The umlauted vowels have a two letter alternative spelling.
Volapükaeiouäöü
IPA/a//e//i//o//u//ɛ//ø//y/
Alt.aeiouaeoeue
Two consecutive vowels can be pronounced together as a diphtong, or separately, at the discretion of the speaker.
This rule also applies in the case of the alternate pronounciation of umlauted vowels.
E.g.: "pük" can be alternatively written as "puek" and pronounced /pyk/, /pu.ek/, /pwek/ or /pujk/

Volapük has 19 consonants, some of them have alternate pronounciations.
Volapükbcdfghjklmnprstvxyz
IPA/b//tʃ/ or /dʒ//d//f//g//h//ʃ/ or /ʒ//k//l//m//n//p//r/ or /ɾ//s/ or /z//t//v//ks/ or /gz//j//ts/ or /dz/
Stress in Volapük falls always on the last syllable.


2 - Roots

Roots are the basic building block of a word in Volapük, denoting primary notions and are free of affixes.

There are three type of roots: nominal, numeric and prepositional.
E.g.: buk (book) - nominal root, bal (one) - numeric root, nin (inside) - prepositional root.
Another example of a nominal root would be "grün" (green) - the basic meaning refers to the color, but often it is used as an adjective ("grünik")

Volapük's phonology requires that a root always begins and ends in a consonant.
Roots can be joined together to form composite words, or can be modified by prefixes and suffixes. See rule 10 - Affixes


3 - Nouns

The noun in Volapük has two numbers: singular and plural. The plural is marked with the ending -s.
E.g.: buk (book) - buks (books)

There are four cases in Volapük: nominative, accusative, dative and genitive:
casesingularpluralusage
nominative- (root form)*-(i)sBuk binon is. (The book is here.)
accusative-i-isLogob buki. (I see the book.)
dative:-e-esGivob buke tegi nulik. (I give the book a new cover.)
genitive:-a-asSubyek buka binon nitedik. (The subject of the book is interesting.)

Four aditional situations that can be interpreted as cases exist:
- predicative: -u - Välons oni presidanu (= as presidan) (They elected him president)
- locative (as adverb): -o: Erivobs domo (= a dom). (We arrived at home)
- ablative: -ao - Erivons domao (= se dom) - They arrived from the house.
- allative: -io - Muvons domio (= lu dom) - They are moving towards the house.

There are no genders in Volapük Fasilik. A noun in its basic form is inherently neuter or of unspecified gender.
A word can take the prefix ji- to be marked as explicitly feminine and hi- to be marked as explicitly masculine.

There are no definite or indefinite articles. The article el can be used to introduce quotations or foreign names. It is inflected according to case as well:
E.g.: At binon magot ela London (this is an image of London)


4 - Adjectives

Adjectives follow the noun and they end in the ending -ik
The comparative of adjectives is formed with the suffix -um or with the adverb plu
The superlative of adjectives is formed with the suffix -ün (-uen) or with the adverb leplu
E.g.: red (red) > redik (red - adj.); redikum/plu redik (more red); redik


5 - Numerals

Cardinal numerals:
ser (0), bal (1), tel (2), kil (3), fol (4), lul (5), mäl (6), vel (7), jöl (8), zül (9)
deg (10), *sent (100), mil (1000), milion (1000000)

Other numerals can be formed with the following suffixes:
- ordinal numerals (first, second, etc.): -id: balid, telid, kilid ..
- multiplicatives (simple, double, etc.): -ik: balik, telik, kilik ..
- iteratives (once, twice, etc): -na: balna, telna, kilna ..
- fractions (1/n): -im: balim (haf) (1/2), kilim (1/3), degim (1/10)

7 - Verbs

Verbs in Volapük are inflected for: tense, person and mood.
The indicative present is formed by suffixing the personal pronouns to the verb root:
-ob: (Ist sg.) lernob
-ol: (IInd sg.) lernol
-on: (IIIrd sg.) lernon
-obs: (Ist pl.) lernobs
-ols: (IInd pl.) lernols
-ons: (IIIrd pl.) lernons

Other tenses receive a prefix: present: a- (in practice, the present tense prefix is omitted)
past: e- elernob
past perfect: i- ilernob
future: o- olernob
future in the past: u- ulernob

Other moods are attached at the end:
interrogative: -li lernob-li? (do i learn?)
conditional: -la lernob-la (i would learn)
imperative: -o lernobso! (let us learn!)
The conditional can be combined with the interrogative: lernob-lai?


8 - Other parts of speech

Adverbs determine a verb and take the ending -o
You can make an adjective into an adverb by adding -o to the final -ik: gudik (good) > gudiko (well)
Prepositions, conjunctions and interjections are either primary or derived.
Derived prepositions and conjunctions are formed from the root with the ending -u, and interjections are formed with -o
As a shortcut, an interrogative command can be expressed as an interjection: lerno! (equivalent to lernolo!)


9 - Demonstratives and correlatives

Demonstratives: at (this), et (that), it (self), ot (same), ut (the one that..)

Correlatives
- person (who): kin
- object (what): kis
- place (where): kiplado
- mode (how): vio
- quantity (how much): liomödo
- time (when): ven (-tim)

These can be combined with: som (some), an (any), ne (no), etc. to form relative pronouns and adverbs.
E.g.: Somtimo (sometimes), anplaso (anywhere), soman (someone), anan/ek (anyone - this one has a separate form)


10 - Affixes

Volapük has a rich system of affixes to form new meaning from existing roots:


Prefixes:

hi-: masculine
ji-: feminine
lex-: former, ex-
le-: augmentative
lu-: of lesser status
mi-: bad, derogative
ne-: negative, opposite
vi-: vice


Adverbial prefixes:

beno-: good, positive
re-: repetition


Prepositional prefixes:

bai-: according to, agreement, con-*
de- (de, dö): from, about
di-: disassemble, undo, dis-
fo-: in front of, ante-*
ge-: reciprocation, back
ko(n)- (ko): with, together, community, con-*
love-: (love): over
ni(n)- (in, ini): inside, interior, in-
nen-: without, lacking, a-
pos-: after, post-
pre-: before, ante-*, pre-
sub- (sub): under, sub-
ta-: against, anti-, contra-
vu-: between, amongst, inter-
ze-: in the middle, center
zu-: around, circum-


Suffixes:

-ad: a related notion, a generalization
-am: action

-ar: a container for something
