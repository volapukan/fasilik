Welcome to the Easy Volapük page!
Volapük was created by Johann Martin Schleyer in 1879 and was the first international auxiliary language to gain widespread popularity.
Language clubs appeared all over the world and there have been three Volapük Congresses in Friedrichshafen (1884), Munich (1887) and Paris (1889).
The official version of the language in use today is the 1931 revision by Arie de Jong which succeeded to clarify some of the aspects of the original language.
Nowadays, interest for Volapük remains high among language lovers, although the number of actual speakers remains very small.
Volapük Fasil is a variant of the language which aims to make it more accessible and comprehensible for new learners.
It simplifies some of the aspects of original/reformed Volapük, and provides a way to learn the essentials of the language.
Volapük Fasil also wants to be a modern, living language, modelled by its community.
